Estonia

Estonia is a small tech-savvy country in northeastern Europe known for its high-income economy, digital government, and charming capital of Tallinn. It gained independence from the Soviet Union in 1991 and has a unique language and rich cultural heritage. Estonias's natural beauty is also a draw for visitors, with vast forests, pristine lakes, and a long coastline along the Baltic Sea. The country has several national parks and protected areas, offering opportunities for hiking, birdwatching, and other outdoor activities. The capital city, Tallinn, is a popular tourist destination, known for its well-preserved medieval old town and vibrant cultural scene.

Did you know that about Estonia?

  1. Estonia has one of the highest literacy rates in the world, with nearly 100% of its population being able to read and write.
  2. Estonia has the highest number of meteorite craters per land area in the world, with over 400 identified craters.
  3. Estonia is home to the world's first e-Residency program, which allows individuals to establish and manage a business online from anywhere in the world.
  4. Estonians are known for their love of singing, and the country has a rich choral tradition, with large-scale singing festivals held regularly.
  5. Estonia has the most extensive collection of folk songs in the world, with over 133,000 recorded songs.
  6. The Estonian language is unique and unrelated to any other language, with only around 1.1 million speakers worldwide.
  7. Estonia has a high number of islands, with over 2,000 identified islands and islets along its coastline.
  8. Estonia has a long tradition of sauna culture, and there are over 1,200 public saunas throughout the country.
  9. Estonia has a national food called black bread, which is a dense and hearty rye bread that has been a staple of the Estonian diet for centuries.
  10. Estonia has a thriving startup scene and has been called the “Silicon Valley of Europe,” with several successful tech companies, including Skype and TransferWise, having been founded in Estonia.
